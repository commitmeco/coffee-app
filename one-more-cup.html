3 Days In
Oaxaca,
Mexico

Where ancient mole traditions meet single-origin coffee culture. Oaxaca's Sierra Norte highlands are quietly producing some of Mexico's most extraordinary beans — and its cafés know exactly how to serve them.

  • Day 01
    Arrive, Orient, Caffeinate
    Check in to Casa Oaxaca. Breakfast at Boulenc. First pour-over at Café Brujula on Macedonio Alcalá — order the Sierra Norte natural process, sit outside.
  • Day 02
    Farm Visit & Market Immersion
    Dawn ride to Finca El Paraíso, 2,200m. Watch hand-sorting, tour fermentation tanks. Afternoon: Benito Juárez Market for local varietals.
  • Day 03
    Roastery Tour & Mezcal Pairing
    Morning at Origen Tostadores — watch their 12kg Probat at work. Evening: mezcal and coffee cocktail flight at In Situ.

Producer Profile
Q&A
Don Pachi Serracín
5th Generation Coffee Producer · Chiriquí, Panama

"Coffee is
not a product.
It's a language."

At 4,800 feet above the Barú volcano, the Serracín family has been growing Gesha since 1963. We sat with Don Pachi at dawn as workers began the first pick of the season.

OMC: When did you first understand you had something truly special?
It was 2004. We submitted to the Best of Panama auction almost as an afterthought. When that Gesha lot sold for over $21 a pound — three times anything before — I knew we were dealing with something that changed the rules. I sat with my father and we both cried. He had kept these trees alive for forty years without knowing.

"When a buyer in Tokyo tells me they can taste the volcanic soil, the morning mist, the hands that picked — that is the highest compliment in any language."

— Don Pachi Serracín
OMC: How do you think about sustainability at this altitude?
The volcano gives us everything — the drainage, the temperature swings, the minerality. Our job is not to take. We use no synthetic chemicals. Every tree in our forest shade provides habitat and keeps the microclimate stable. Sustainability for us is not marketing. It's survival. If we exhaust this land, we are finished.
