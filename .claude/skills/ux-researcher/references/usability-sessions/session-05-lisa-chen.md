# Usability Session 05 — Lisa Chen

**Date:** April 10, 2026
**Participant:** Lisa Chen, 40, small business owner (bakery)
**Coffee experience:** Serves coffee at her bakery, learning to improve her offerings
**Equipment:** Commercial drip brewer, recently bought a V60 for personal use
**Recruitment source:** Local coffee meetup group
**Moderator:** Emily N.
**Session length:** 36 minutes
**Device:** iPad Air, Safari

---

## Pre-task interview (3 min)

**Moderator:** What brings you to a coffee app?

**Lisa:** I run a bakery and we serve coffee, but it's honestly not great. I want to upgrade — offer specialty coffee — but I don't know where to start. I'm looking for education and maybe sourcing. I need to understand quality so I can choose better beans for my shop and talk to my customers about what we serve.

---

## Task 1: Evaluate the catalog for sourcing (8 min)

**Lisa:** *opens app on iPad* Nice layout on the iPad. Lots of space. *goes straight to catalog*

*applies filters: Score 85+*

**Lisa:** Okay, I can filter by score — that's useful. I need consistent quality. Let me look at these... *taps into a bean*

**Lisa:** CQI Score 87.2. Colombia, Huila. Washed. This looks good. But... what's the lot size? How do I actually source this? Is there a wholesale option?

*scrolls around*

**Lisa:** I see "Add to Cart" for $18.99/12oz. That's consumer pricing. For a bakery I'd need 5-10 pounds a week. There's no wholesale info, no importer listed, no way to contact the producer.

**Moderator:** Is that a dealbreaker?

**Lisa:** Not for learning — the data is amazing for understanding what quality looks like. But for buying, yes. I'd need at least a roaster name or importer. The CQI data tells me the green coffee is good but doesn't help me actually get it.

---

## Task 2: Learn about processing methods (5 min)

**Lisa:** I keep hearing "washed vs natural" and I don't fully understand the difference. Is there anything in the app that explains this?

*looks around bean detail page*

**Lisa:** It says "Processing Method: Washed" but no explanation. *checks brew guide* The brew guide mentions "washed process" too but just in passing — "washed process beans" as a recommendation. No definition.

*checks if there's a glossary, FAQ, or education section*

**Lisa:** There's no learning section? For an app with this much technical data, I'd expect some kind of coffee education page. Even just an FAQ — "What is SCA scoring? What's the difference between washed and natural? What does altitude do to flavor?"

---

## Task 3: Use the app as a training tool (5 min)

**Moderator:** Imagine you're training a new barista. Could this app help?

**Lisa:** *thinks* The brew guide could. If I said "learn how to make a pour-over" I could send them the brew guide page. That's useful.

But for understanding flavor? Not really. The cupping scores are there but there's no tasting exercise or framework. What would be amazing is something like: "Here's a bean. Before you look at the scores, taste it and describe what you notice. Now compare your notes to the cupping scores." Like a self-guided cupping exercise.

**Lisa:** The flavor wheel data is in here somewhere right? But I can't find it anywhere in the UI. It's like the data exists but there's no feature built on top of it.

---

## Task 4: Diary for tracking tasting experiments (5 min)

**Lisa:** *adds several beans to diary with different statuses*

The diary is useful for tracking beans I've tried. But for business use, I'd want to compare them — like a tasting matrix. "I tried five Colombians last week, here's how they scored on MY palate."

*writes a note on one bean*

**Lisa:** The text box is fine for a sentence but I want to rate things myself. Like, give it my own 1-5 on acidity, sweetness, body. Build my own tasting log. A structured diary entry would be way more useful than freeform text.

---

## Post-task debrief (4 min)

**Moderator:** Overall?

**Lisa:** This is the best coffee data I've seen in one place. The CQI scores, the origin data, the cupping breakdowns — it's like having a reference library. But it's missing the educational layer that would make it truly useful. You have all this knowledge and it's locked behind numbers without explanations. If you added a coffee education section and a structured tasting log in the diary, this could be the app that the whole specialty coffee community uses to learn.

**Moderator:** One fix?

**Lisa:** Add a glossary or education section. Even a simple page that explains: what cupping scores mean, how processing affects flavor, what altitude does, and how to taste coffee intentionally. You have the data — help people understand it.

**Moderator:** NPS?

**Lisa:** 7. It's close to being great. The foundation is rock solid.

---

## Observer notes

- Business use case — wanted wholesale/sourcing info, couldn't find it
- No educational content despite having extensive technical data
- Flavor taxonomy exists in data but isn't surfaced in any feature
- Wants structured diary entries (own ratings), not just freeform text
- Suggested self-guided cupping exercises — novel feature idea
- iPad layout was comfortable — app works well on tablet
- Missing glossary is a consistent finding across ALL sessions
- NPS 7 — impressed by data quality, blocked by missing education layer
