# Usability Session 02 — Marcus T.

**Date:** April 8, 2026
**Participant:** Marcus T., 35, software engineer
**Coffee experience:** 5+ years into specialty, roasts at home occasionally
**Equipment:** V60, AeroPress, Comandante grinder, Breville Barista Express espresso machine
**Recruitment source:** r/coffee subreddit
**Moderator:** Emily N.
**Session length:** 34 minutes
**Device:** MacBook Pro, Chrome

---

## Pre-task interview (3 min)

**Moderator:** Tell me about your coffee setup.

**Marcus:** I'm pretty deep in. I have a V60 and an AeroPress for daily, a Breville for espresso on weekends. Comandante C40 grinder. I buy from local roasters mostly — Onyx, Black & White, sometimes Sey. I've done one SCA cupping workshop so I know the basics of formal evaluation.

**Moderator:** How do you discover new beans?

**Marcus:** Honestly, Instagram and word of mouth. I follow roasters, and when someone posts something that looks interesting I'll check it out. I don't really use any apps for it — I've tried a couple but they felt too casual. Like they were made for people who think Starbucks dark roast is good coffee.

---

## Task 1: Explore and find something interesting (7 min)

**Marcus:** *opens app* Dark theme, nice. *immediately looks at filters* Processing Method, Origin, Score... great, these are the right categories. *applies Washed + Ethiopia*

Okay, I've got results. *scans cards* Scores are visible on the cards, that's good. *clicks into a bean at 87.5*

This is solid — I can see all the cupping attributes. Aroma 8.33, Flavor 8.42... *nods* These match what I'd expect from a high-scoring washed Ethiopian. Altitude 2200m, that tracks.

**Marcus:** But here's my issue — where's the roaster? Where's the roast date? Where can I buy this? It says "Find This Bean Near You" but... *clicks* ...this just shows coffee shops on a map? These are generic shops, not places that carry this specific bean. That's misleading.

**Moderator:** What did you expect?

**Marcus:** A link to buy it. Or at least the roaster's name so I can look them up. The CQI data is green coffee scores — I get that, it's the raw bean quality. But as a consumer, I need to know who roasted it and whether I can actually get it. Otherwise it's like a museum — interesting to look at but I can't take anything home.

---

## Task 2: Use the diary (5 min)

**Marcus:** *adds a bean to diary as "Want to Try"*

Okay, saved. *goes to diary page* Good, I can see it here. Can I add tasting notes? *taps into the bean from diary*

Oh, the note is buried in the detail page. I'd want to log my brew — what method, what ratio, what I tasted. Right now it's just a freeform text box. No structure at all.

**Marcus:** For me, a tasting log needs: date, brew method, dose and ratio, grind setting, and then the tasting notes. Just a text box isn't enough — I'd end up using a spreadsheet anyway, which defeats the purpose.

---

## Task 3: Take the quiz (4 min)

**Marcus:** *starts quiz* "Discover your coffee personality in 60 seconds." Sure, let's see.

*goes through questions quickly*

**Marcus:** These questions are fine for beginners. For me they're a bit... broad. "How do you feel about acidity?" I mean, it depends on the coffee. An acidic Kenya and an acidic Colombian are totally different things.

*gets results: "The Connoisseur"*

**Marcus:** *reads profile* Okay, this is flattering but vague. "You appreciate complexity and nuance." Sure. The recommended beans — *checks scores* — these are all 85+ which is fine. But the recommendations feel random. I'd rather filter by specific attributes than take a personality quiz.

**Moderator:** Would you use the quiz again?

**Marcus:** No, once was enough. But I can see it being useful for someone just starting out. It's a good on-ramp — just not for me.

---

## Task 4: Try the brew guide (6 min)

**Marcus:** *navigates to brew guide* Pour-over, French press, AeroPress... these are the right methods.

*taps pour-over*

**Marcus:** *reads through* 200-205°F, 1:16 ratio, bloom at 30 seconds... this is accurate. *reads steps* "Start your timer. Pour just enough water to wet all the grounds — this is the bloom. Wait 30 seconds." Good, bloom is correct.

"Best for these beans: Light and medium roasts, washed process beans..." Yeah, that's right.

**Marcus:** What I'd want to see here that's missing — grind size in microns or clicks, not just "medium-fine." "Medium-fine" means different things on different grinders. And water temperature in Celsius by default — or at least toggle-able. But the copy is solid, whoever wrote this actually knows coffee.

**Moderator:** Anything else about the brew guide?

**Marcus:** It should link back to the beans. If I'm reading the pour-over guide and it says "great for washed Ethiopians," show me the washed Ethiopians in the catalog. Right now these are separate islands — the brew guide doesn't connect to the beans and the beans don't connect to the brew guide.

---

## Task 5: Try to compare beans (5 min)

**Marcus:** *searches for comparison feature* There's no compare feature, right? *looks around* No. That's a big miss. When I'm choosing between two beans from the same origin, I want to see the cupping scores side by side.

Let me try something... *opens two beans in separate tabs* Okay, I can do it manually. But the radar charts would be great overlaid. Like, show me two flavor profiles on one radar chart so I can see where they differ.

---

## Post-task debrief (4 min)

**Moderator:** Overall?

**Marcus:** The data quality is the best I've seen in a coffee app. 1,338 beans with full CQI cupping data — that's impressive. The design is clean and the dark mode is well done. But it's stuck between being a data tool and a discovery tool. For someone like me, I want to DO things with the data — compare, filter more granularly, log structured brews. For beginners, there's not enough context around what the data means.

**Moderator:** One thing to change?

**Marcus:** Connect the brew guide to the beans. When I'm looking at a washed Ethiopian light roast, tell me "pour-over will bring out the florals" and link to the guide. That's the kind of intelligent recommendation that would make this app essential rather than nice-to-have.

**Moderator:** Recommendation score, 0-10?

**Marcus:** 8 for fellow coffee people. 5 for my wife who just started.

---

## Observer notes

- Expert user — navigated confidently, used filters immediately
- "Find This Bean Near You" is misleading — expects purchase links, gets generic shops
- Wants structured tasting logs, not freeform text
- Quiz too basic for experienced users but recognized it's good for beginners
- Strongly wants bean ↔ brew guide cross-linking
- Wants comparison feature with overlaid radar charts
- Grind size needs more specificity ("medium-fine" is ambiguous)
- Split NPS recommendation (8 for enthusiasts, 5 for beginners) — interesting signal
