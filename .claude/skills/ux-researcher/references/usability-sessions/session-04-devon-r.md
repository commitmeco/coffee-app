# Usability Session 04 — Devon R.

**Date:** April 9, 2026
**Participant:** Devon R., 22, college student
**Coffee experience:** Daily coffee drinker, mostly cafe orders, just bought first grinder
**Equipment:** Hario Skerton hand grinder (new), borrowed Chemex from roommate
**Recruitment source:** University bulletin board
**Moderator:** Emily N.
**Session length:** 32 minutes
**Device:** iPhone 13 mini, Safari

---

## Pre-task interview (2 min)

**Moderator:** How do you usually drink coffee?

**Devon:** I get an oat milk latte almost every day from this place near campus. But I just bought a grinder because I want to start making coffee at home to save money. My roommate has a Chemex he never uses so I figured I'd try it. I literally haven't made my own pour-over yet.

---

## Task 1: Homepage and first impressions (5 min)

**Devon:** *opens on phone* This looks sick, actually. Very clean. *scrolls* Editor's Picks... *swipes through cards* These cards are nice on mobile, I can swipe through them.

*taps a bean*

**Devon:** Guatemala, 84.5. "Very Good" — oh cool, there's a label! That's helpful. *scrolls* Cupping scores... these are interesting but I don't know what most of them mean. What's "Uniformity"?

*scrolls to "Find This Bean Near You"*

**Devon:** Oh, shops! *taps* Wait, these are just coffee shops in general? I thought these would be places that sell this exact bean. Like, can I buy this bean? *looks for buy button* Oh, there's "Add to Cart" — nice. $18.99 for 12oz... that's pricey but okay.

---

## Task 2: Find something specific — "I want something for my Chemex" (6 min)

**Devon:** So I have a Chemex, which I think is a pour-over? Let me see if there's a brew guide... *taps Brew Guide in nav*

**Devon:** Pour-over, French press... the Chemex is pour-over right? *taps pour-over* "Dripper (V60, Kalita, Chemex)" — yes! Okay. "Light and medium roasts, washed process beans, single-origin coffees."

*reads "Best for these beans"*

**Devon:** So I should look for light roast, washed... *goes back to catalog* Let me filter. *opens filters on mobile*

**Devon:** Um... *taps filter icon* Oh, the filters are here. *scrolls through filter options* I see Processing Method. *taps Washed* And... I don't see a "roast level" filter? Just processing method, origin, and scores. How do I find light roast?

**Moderator:** Is there a roast level in the data?

**Devon:** *checks a bean detail page* No, I don't think so. The brew guide says "light and medium roasts" but the app doesn't have roast level as a filter or data point. That's confusing.

---

## Task 3: Quiz and discovery (5 min)

**Devon:** *takes quiz on mobile*

The questions are easy to tap. *goes through quickly* Oh wait, Q4 — I have to hit Continue? Every other one just went. *taps Continue*

*gets "The Explorer"*

**Devon:** *reads* "You want variety." True! The beans look good... *tries to swipe through them* They're in a grid, not a carousel. On my phone I have to scroll. The cards are a bit squeezed — I can see two per row but the text is small.

*tries to save a recommended bean*

**Devon:** Can I save one of these? *taps a bean card* Oh, it takes me to the detail page. Then I save from there. So I leave the quiz results to save a bean. Can I get back to my results? *hits back* Oh good, they're still there.

---

## Task 4: Social features (4 min)

**Devon:** *finds Social in nav* Oh, there's a community! *scrolls feed*

"@CoffeeNerd42 tried Ethiopian Yirgacheffe..." Cool. But who are these people? Are these real users? It feels kind of empty — like there's activity but no way to interact. Can I follow someone? *looks* No. Can I comment? No. Can I share something myself?

**Devon:** It's like a read-only social feed? That's kinda weird. Either make it interactive or don't call it Social. Right now it feels like a demo.

---

## Post-task debrief (4 min)

**Moderator:** Overall?

**Devon:** I think it's really cool for what it is. The brew guide alone is worth it — I literally learned that the Chemex is a pour-over from this app. But on mobile there are some squished spots, especially the quiz results cards. And the social page needs work — it's either a real social feature or it shouldn't exist.

**Moderator:** One fix?

**Devon:** The brew guide should link directly to beans I can buy for that method. "Here's how to do pour-over, and here are the best beans for it — add to cart." That's a complete experience.

**Moderator:** NPS score?

**Devon:** 7. Once they add more interactive stuff and fix the mobile layout I'd go higher.

---

## Observer notes

- Mobile-first user — tested everything on small screen
- Quiz results cards too cramped on iPhone 13 mini (260px card width)
- Multi-select Continue confusion (Q4) — consistent across all participants
- Social feed feels "read-only demo" — no interaction, no authenticity cues
- Brew guide → beans connection is the #1 request across sessions
- Didn't know Chemex was pour-over — brew guide educated him (positive)
- No roast level data/filter — gap between brew guide advice and catalog capability
- "Find This Bean Near You" misunderstood — expected purchase link, got generic shops
