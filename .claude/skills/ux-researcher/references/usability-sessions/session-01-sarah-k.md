# Usability Session 01 — Sarah K.

**Date:** April 8, 2026
**Participant:** Sarah K., 28, marketing coordinator
**Coffee experience:** 6 months into specialty coffee, has a subscription to Trade Coffee
**Equipment:** Drip machine, recently bought a French press
**Recruitment source:** Instagram ad for beta testers
**Moderator:** Emily N.
**Session length:** 38 minutes
**Device:** iPhone 14, Safari

---

## Pre-task interview (3 min)

**Moderator:** Tell me a bit about your coffee routine.

**Sarah:** I started getting into coffee maybe six months ago? My boyfriend got me a Trade subscription for my birthday and I was like, oh, this is way better than Folgers. So now I'm kind of obsessed but I still feel like I don't really know what I'm doing. I just know I like some beans more than others but I can't always explain why.

**Moderator:** When you get a new bag, how do you decide if you like it?

**Sarah:** Honestly I just... drink it? And if it's good I try to remember the name. I've started taking photos of the bags I like so I can reorder them. But sometimes I forget what I liked about them specifically.

**Moderator:** Have you used any coffee apps before?

**Sarah:** No, I didn't even know they existed. I looked at some stuff on Reddit but it was really intimidating. People talking about extraction yields and TDS and I was like, nope.

---

## Task 1: Find a coffee you'd want to try (8 min)

**Moderator:** I'm going to show you a coffee app. Spend a minute looking around, and then try to find a coffee you'd want to try.

**Sarah:** *opens app* Oh, this is pretty. I like the dark theme. Okay... "Discover Amazing Coffee." Hmm. *scrolls down* Editor's Picks... okay, these look nice. *taps a bean card* Oh wait, I accidentally tapped... okay no, that's what I wanted, the detail page.

*on bean detail page*

**Sarah:** Country: Ethiopia. Processing: Washed. I know Ethiopia is supposed to be good? And I think washed means... something about water? I don't totally know. Total CQI Score: 86.92. Is that good? Like, what's the scale? Is it out of 100?

**Moderator:** What would help you understand that?

**Sarah:** Maybe just like... a little label? Like "excellent" or something? Or a scale showing where it falls? Because 86 out of 100 sounds pretty good but out of 1000 it's terrible, you know? *laughs*

*scrolls to cupping scores*

**Sarah:** Aroma: 8.17. Flavor: 8.25. These are all around 8. Is 8 good? I'm guessing yes? But I literally have no frame of reference. *pauses* I feel dumb saying this but I don't know what "Clean Cup" means as a score. Like, is my cup dirty?

**Moderator:** You're not dumb at all — that's really helpful feedback. Would you try this bean?

**Sarah:** Yeah, maybe? But I can't tell from the page what it actually *tastes* like in words I understand. The numbers don't translate to "this tastes like blueberry" for me.

---

## Task 2: Take the flavor quiz (6 min)

**Moderator:** Can you find the quiz?

**Sarah:** *looks at nav* Beans, Shops, Quiz... oh there it is with the sparkle. *taps*

"Discover your coffee personality in 60 seconds." Cute, I like that. *taps Let's Go*

*goes through questions*

**Sarah:** "How do you feel about bright, tangy acidity?" Um, I honestly don't know? I think I like it? *selects option, auto-advances* Oh! It just moved on. Okay. That's fast.

*on Q4, flavor notes multi-select*

**Sarah:** Fruity, floral, sweet... I'll pick fruity and sweet. *taps both, waits* Why isn't it moving? Oh, I need to hit Continue? The other ones just went automatically. That's confusing.

*on results page*

**Sarah:** "The Sweetheart" — okay, that's fun. *reads description* "You're drawn to sweetness and comfort." Yeah, that sounds right! And... "Beans We Think You'll Love" — oh these look good! But... *scrolls* ...wait, how do I save just one of these? There's only "Buy Your Sweetheart Bundle." I don't want to buy all four. I just want to remember this one Ethiopian one.

**Moderator:** What would you want to do with it?

**Sarah:** Save it somewhere? Like a wishlist? Oh wait, is that what the diary is? *navigates to diary* It's empty. I think I need to go back to the bean and add it from there? That's a lot of steps from the quiz result.

---

## Task 3: Save a bean to your diary (5 min)

**Moderator:** Try saving a bean to your diary.

**Sarah:** *goes to catalog, taps a bean* Okay, I'm on the detail page... *scrolls* Oh! There's like a little bookmark icon? Or... *finds save button* "Want to Try" — yes! *taps* Okay it seems like it saved? Oh there's a little animation. Nice. Let me check the diary... *navigates* Yes! It's here! And I can see the status. Cool.

But wait — I wrote a note on another bean earlier in my head, where do I write that? *taps the bean in diary* Oh, I have to go all the way into the bean detail again. I wish I could just jot a note right here on the diary page. Like "this was the blueberry one."

---

## Task 4: Find the brew guide (5 min)

**Moderator:** Let's say you want to learn how to brew one of these beans at home. Can you find help with that?

**Sarah:** Hmm... *looks at nav* Beans, Shops, Brew Guide, Quiz, Diary... Brew Guide! *taps*

Oh nice, there are different methods. French press, pour-over... I have a French press! *taps French press*

**Sarah:** "The no-fuss classic." I love that. And the steps are really clear. 1, 2, 3... "Pour immediately — don't let it sit in the press, or it'll keep extracting and turn bitter." Oh my god, I've been leaving it in the press! That explains so much!

*scrolls to "Best for these beans"*

**Sarah:** "Medium and dark roasts, natural process beans, anything with chocolate, nut, or caramel notes." This is SO helpful. I wish the bean pages linked to this. Like if I'm looking at a bean, it could say "try this with a French press."

**Moderator:** That's a great idea. Anything else?

**Sarah:** Maybe a video? Like, I learn better watching someone do it. But the written steps are solid too.

---

## Task 5: Compare two beans (4 min)

**Moderator:** Let's say you're trying to decide between two beans. How would you compare them?

**Sarah:** *goes to catalog, opens a bean* Okay... so I'd look at this one... and then I'd need to go back and open another one? And try to remember what the first one said?

*tries opening two beans in tabs*

**Sarah:** I guess I could open them in two tabs? But that's not really a feature, that's me hacking around it. It would be nice to have a side-by-side. Like, "compare these two." Even just the scores next to each other would help.

---

## Post-task debrief (4 min)

**Moderator:** Overall impressions?

**Sarah:** I really like it. It's beautiful, the quiz was fun, and the brew guide is exactly what I need. But I feel like it's made for people who already know about coffee. The scores need context — tell me if 86 is good! The flavor notes on the bean page don't mean anything to me yet. I'd keep using it but I need more hand-holding to get started.

**Moderator:** If you could change one thing?

**Sarah:** Make the quiz results actually connect to saving beans. I found my profile, saw beans I liked, but then couldn't save them or find them again easily. The quiz should be the front door for people like me.

**Moderator:** How likely would you recommend this to a friend, 0-10?

**Sarah:** 7. I'd say "it's a cool app if you're into coffee, but bring your patience if you're new."

---

## Observer notes

- Consistently confused by cupping scores — needs context at every level
- Multi-select → Continue confusion on quiz Q4 (hesitated 5+ seconds)
- Wanted to save individual beans from quiz results, couldn't
- Strong positive reaction to brew guide copy ("this explains so much")
- Wanted brew method recommendations on bean detail pages
- Diary note-taking requires too many taps — wants inline notes
- Never found the search bar — scrolled instead
