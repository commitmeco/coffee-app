# Usability Session 03 — Priya M.

**Date:** April 9, 2026
**Participant:** Priya M., 31, product designer
**Coffee experience:** Mostly a tea drinker, trying to get into coffee
**Equipment:** Mr. Coffee drip machine (partner's), occasionally buys lattes
**Recruitment source:** Friend referral
**Moderator:** Emily N.
**Session length:** 35 minutes
**Device:** Samsung Galaxy S23, Chrome

---

## Pre-task interview (3 min)

**Moderator:** What's your relationship with coffee?

**Priya:** I'm a tea person. Like, I know tea. But my partner is really into coffee and I'm trying to understand it better so we can share that. Right now I just order lattes and sometimes I'll try his pour-over and it's either fine or it's too sour and I don't know why.

**Moderator:** What would you want from a coffee app?

**Priya:** Honestly? Help me understand what I like and why. I don't know the terminology. Like when someone says "bright and fruity" — what does that actually taste like? Is that the sour one?

---

## Task 1: First impressions and orientation (6 min)

**Priya:** *opens app* Ooh, nice design. Very moody. "Discover Amazing Coffee." Okay.

*scrolls slowly, looking at everything*

**Priya:** Editor's Picks... these cards look nice. *reads a card* "Ethiopia, Yirgacheffe, 87.5." I don't know what Yirgacheffe is. Is that a bean? A place? *taps card*

*on detail page*

**Priya:** Processing Method: Washed. Altitude: 2200 masl. *long pause* I don't know what any of this means. MASL? Is that a unit? And "Washed" — is that how they clean the bean? Does it taste different if it's washed?

*scrolls to cupping scores*

**Priya:** Aroma: 8.17. I don't know if that's a lot. *scrolls more* Clean Cup: 10.0. Sweetness: 10.0. Oh, is 10 the max? So this one is perfectly sweet? But when I had Ethiopian coffee it was sour, not sweet. I'm confused.

**Moderator:** What would help?

**Priya:** Tooltips. Like if I tap on "Washed" it tells me what that means. Or if I tap on the score it says "8.17 out of 10 — this is really good, most specialty beans score between 7 and 9." Just... context.

---

## Task 2: Find something you'd like (5 min)

**Moderator:** Try to find a coffee you think you'd enjoy.

**Priya:** *looks at filters* Hmm... I could filter by... origin? I don't know which origins I like. Flavor? *opens flavor filter* "Fruity, Floral, Sweet, Nutty/Cocoa..." Okay, I think I like sweet? And nutty? Those sound safe.

*applies Sweet + Nutty/Cocoa*

**Priya:** *scrolls results* These all have scores but no descriptions. I can see "Colombia, Huila" but I don't know if that means it's sweet just because I filtered for sweet. Where are the flavor descriptions? Oh wait — I have to click into each one to see?

*clicks a bean*

**Priya:** I don't see a flavor description. Just the numbers. Where does it say "this tastes like chocolate and caramel"? The filter had "Nutty/Cocoa" but the bean page doesn't mention those words. How do I know this is actually what I want?

**Moderator:** That's a really important observation.

**Priya:** It feels like the filter labels and the bean pages are speaking different languages.

---

## Task 3: Take the quiz (5 min)

**Priya:** *finds quiz in nav* "Discover your coffee personality in 60 seconds." Okay, yes, this is more my speed.

*takes quiz — selects "smooth" for taste, low acidity, medium body, Sweet + Nutty-Cocoa, low adventure, Morning*

**Priya:** "The Comforter" — *reads description* "You want coffee to feel like a warm hug." Aww. Yes! That's exactly right.

*looks at recommended beans*

**Priya:** These look good! But... *reads bean cards* ...they just show the scores again. Not why they were recommended for me. Like, this one is 85.67 from Brazil — cool, but why is it a "Comforter" bean? Does it taste like a warm hug?

**Moderator:** What would you want to see there?

**Priya:** Something like "We picked this because it's smooth, low-acid, with chocolate and nut notes — exactly your Comforter profile." Match the recommendation to the quiz words I already understand.

---

## Task 4: Explore the brew guide (5 min)

**Priya:** *taps brew guide* Oh I haven't seen this page before. French press — that's what we have!

*reads French press guide*

**Priya:** "The no-fuss classic." Good, because I am no-fuss. *reads steps* Oh, I didn't know you're supposed to pour immediately! We always leave it sitting there. *reads "Best for these beans"* "Medium and dark roasts, natural process beans, anything with chocolate, nut, or caramel notes."

Wait. "Natural process." Is that different from washed? *looks for explanation* There's no tooltip or explanation here either. The brew guide says these words like I should know them.

**Moderator:** Would you use this guide?

**Priya:** Definitely. The steps are clear and I love the ratio card. But I'd need a glossary or something for the terms. Even just one sentence — "Natural process means the bean is dried inside the fruit, which makes it taste fruitier and sweeter."

---

## Task 5: Use the diary (4 min)

**Priya:** *goes to diary* It's empty. "Your diary is waiting for its first entry." Cute. *taps Browse Beans*

*back in catalog, adds a bean as "Want to Try"*

**Priya:** Okay, saved. *goes back to diary* There it is! "Want to Try." But... does marking something as "Want to Try" do anything? Like, will it remind me? Will it affect my recommendations?

**Moderator:** What would you expect it to do?

**Priya:** At minimum, I'd expect the app to show me my "Want to Try" beans in one place — like a shopping list. Even better, connect it to the shops page — "this bean is available near you" — or send me a notification when it goes on sale. Right now it just... sits there?

---

## Post-task debrief (4 min)

**Moderator:** Overall thoughts?

**Priya:** I think the app is beautifully designed. Like, as a designer myself, I appreciate the craft. But it assumes I know more than I do. Every page has information without explanation. I need the app to teach me while I use it — not in a condescending way, but just... explain the words. If you added tooltips for every technical term and a one-line flavor description for every bean, I'd use this every day.

**Moderator:** One thing to fix first?

**Priya:** The gap between the quiz and the catalog. The quiz speaks my language — "how do you feel about acidity?" — but then the catalog speaks a different language — "Aroma: 8.17." Bridge those two worlds.

**Moderator:** Recommendation score?

**Priya:** 6. It's beautiful and I want to use it, but right now I'd need my partner sitting next to me to explain everything.

---

## Observer notes

- True beginner — stumbled on almost every technical term
- "MASL" completely opaque — didn't even know it was an abbreviation
- Filter labels don't match bean detail page content — disconnect
- Quiz is the ideal entry point for this persona (self-selected into it immediately)
- Quiz results lack the "why" — doesn't explain match rationale
- "Want to Try" feels inert — no downstream effects visible
- Wants tooltips/glossary for all coffee terminology
- Explicitly noted the language gap between quiz (plain language) and catalog (technical)
- Strong positive reaction to brew guide copy style
- NPS 6 — loves the design, blocked by knowledge gap
