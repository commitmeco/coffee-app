# User Interview 02 — Rachel S.

**Date:** April 7, 2026
**Participant:** Rachel S., 38, nurse, mother of two
**Coffee experience:** Daily coffee drinker, interested in upgrading from supermarket coffee
**Interview type:** Semi-structured, 40 minutes, Zoom
**Interviewer:** Emily N.

---

## Coffee routine

**Interviewer:** What does coffee look like in your day?

**Rachel:** It's survival, honestly. *laughs* I work night shifts and I have two kids, so coffee is non-negotiable. I make a pot when I wake up, usually around 2pm for me because of my schedule, drink two cups, and bring one in a thermos to work. On my days off I'll sometimes go to a nice cafe as a treat, and that's where I've realized the coffee I make at home isn't great.

**Interviewer:** What kind of coffee do you usually buy?

**Rachel:** Whatever's on sale at the grocery store, honestly. Dunkin' or Peet's usually. But last month I got a bag from a local roaster and it was noticeably better. It was like, oh, this is a different thing entirely. So now I'm curious but I don't have the time or energy to become a coffee expert.

**Interviewer:** What's holding you back?

**Rachel:** Time. And intimidation. I walked into a specialty coffee shop once and the barista started talking about "third wave" and "single origin" and I felt like I was in a different country. I just wanted something that tasted good. I don't want to study coffee, I just want to drink better coffee.

---

## Decision-making

**Interviewer:** When you're at the grocery store or online, how do you choose a bag?

**Rachel:** The bag design, honestly. And the tasting notes on the back. If it says "chocolate and caramel" I'm interested. If it says "stone fruit and jasmine" I'm skeptical — that doesn't sound like coffee to me. I know that's probably me being narrow-minded but that's where I am.

**Interviewer:** Have you ever looked at cupping scores?

**Rachel:** I don't even know what that is. *hears explanation* Oh, like a wine score? That's interesting. If I saw a number I'd probably look at it the same way I look at wine ratings — above 85 means it's good, below 80 maybe skip it. But I'd need that translated for me. Don't just give me 86.4 — tell me what that means.

---

## App expectations

**Interviewer:** What would make a coffee app useful to you?

**Rachel:** Three things. One — tell me what to buy based on what I already like. I like chocolate and caramel flavors. Don't make me learn the SCA wheel, just recommend beans that taste like what I described. Two — tell me how to make it at home without buying a bunch of equipment. I have a drip machine and nothing else. Three — don't make me feel dumb. Every coffee resource I've found assumes I already know things.

**Interviewer:** What about tracking or journaling your coffee?

**Rachel:** Honestly? I wouldn't track daily brews — that's too much. But if I try a new bean and love it, I'd want to save it so I can remember the name later. A wishlist more than a diary. "Beans I've loved" and "beans I want to try." That's it. Simple.

**Interviewer:** We have a diary feature with those exact statuses — "Loved," "Tried," "Want to Try."

**Rachel:** Oh perfect. But don't call it a "diary" — that sounds like a commitment. "My beans" or "saved beans" feels lighter. A diary sounds like I need to write in it every day.

---

## Content preferences

**Interviewer:** How do you prefer to learn new things?

**Rachel:** Short-form content. I don't have time for long articles. Give me a 30-second explanation. Or better yet, show me a comparison — "this $12 supermarket coffee vs this $18 specialty coffee, here's the difference." Help me understand WHY it's worth paying more, in terms I already get.

**Interviewer:** Would you watch a video or read a guide?

**Rachel:** Short video, definitely. But a well-written guide works too if it's not too long. Bullet points, not paragraphs. I scan, I don't read.

---

## Concerns

**Interviewer:** Any worries about using an app like this?

**Rachel:** Price. Specialty coffee is expensive and I'm feeding a family. If the app only has $18-22 bags I'll feel priced out. Having some budget-friendly recommendations would make it more inclusive. Not everyone can spend $20 on a bag of coffee.

Also — I don't want to feel judged. If I say I like dark roast with cream and sugar, don't make me feel like that's wrong. Every coffee person I've met has opinions and I don't want the app to lecture me.
