# User Interview 03 — Tom H.

**Date:** April 10, 2026
**Participant:** Tom H., 45, high school teacher, coffee club advisor
**Coffee experience:** 8+ years, runs a coffee club with students
**Interview type:** Semi-structured, 42 minutes, in person
**Interviewer:** Emily N.

---

## Background

**Interviewer:** Tell me about the coffee club.

**Tom:** I started it five years ago. We have about 15 kids who meet every Friday and we explore different coffees together — origins, processing methods, basic cupping. The point is to teach critical thinking through sensory evaluation. You taste something, you describe it, you compare it, you defend your opinion. It's the best part of my week.

**Interviewer:** How do you source material for the club?

**Tom:** I buy 3-4 bags a week from local roasters and online. I keep a spreadsheet of everything we've tried — origin, process, roast date, our collective tasting notes, and a 1-10 group rating. It's about 200 rows now. I also print out the SCA flavor wheel for every session.

---

## App as educational tool

**Interviewer:** Could you see using a coffee app with the club?

**Tom:** Maybe. What would be useful is cupping data that we can compare against our own tasting. Like, "the professional cuppers scored this 8.5 for acidity — do you agree?" That's a great teaching moment. It helps the kids calibrate their palates.

**Interviewer:** What about the quiz feature?

**Tom:** For the kids? They'd love it. It's a fun way to start the semester — "find your coffee personality." Then we can group students by profile and have them try beans that match their profile vs. beans outside their comfort zone. That's pedagogy right there.

**Interviewer:** What about the brew guide?

**Tom:** Essential. We use V60s in the club and I'm always explaining the process to new members. If the app had a step-by-step pour-over guide with ratios and timing, I could just say "follow the app." That saves me 15 minutes every session.

---

## Data and tracking

**Interviewer:** You mentioned a spreadsheet. What's in it?

**Tom:** Origin, country, region, processing method, roaster, roast date, our group score, and a column for notable flavors. I also track which students were present and who said what — but that's in a separate doc.

**Interviewer:** Would you switch from the spreadsheet to an app?

**Tom:** Only if the app could do what the spreadsheet does. I need: group ratings (not just individual), the ability to add custom fields, and export to CSV for when I make my end-of-year report. Most apps assume one user per account — I need collaborative features.

**Interviewer:** The diary feature currently supports one user per device.

**Tom:** That's a dealbreaker for group use. Unless... could students each use it on their own phones and then we compare? But there's no way to share a collection or a tasting session, right?

---

## Feature priorities

**Interviewer:** If you could prioritize features for educational use, what would be top 3?

**Tom:** One — cupping score context. When I show a student an 8.5 for acidity, they need to know what that means. A scale from "this is low" to "this is very high" with an example bean at each level. Benchmarking.

Two — side-by-side bean comparison. We always cup two or three beans at once and compare. A comparison view is fundamental to coffee education.

Three — the SCA flavor wheel as an interactive tool. I print it out every week. If it was in the app and students could tap a flavor, see which beans in the catalog have that flavor, and explore from there — that's a discovery tool AND a teaching tool.

---

## Concerns

**Interviewer:** Any worries?

**Tom:** Content moderation if there's a social feature. My students are mostly minors — I need to know the social feed is appropriate. Also, ads. If the app starts pushing sponsored beans, I lose trust and I can't use it in an educational setting.

The other thing — please don't gamify learning with streaks or points. "You've cupped 5 beans this week!" That's not the point. The point is thoughtful engagement, not volume.
