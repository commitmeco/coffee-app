# Sample User Feedback — Coffee App

Collected from a mix of moderated usability sessions, a beta feedback survey, and informal Slack messages from early testers. These are representative, not exhaustive.

---

## Usability Sessions (5 participants, 30 min each)

### P1 — Sarah, 28, coffee subscription customer, new to specialty
**Task: Find a bean similar to one she liked**
- Landed on homepage, scrolled past the hero quickly ("I already know I'm here for coffee")
- Went straight to the catalog, used the search bar — typed "fruity" but search only matches bean names, not flavor notes. "Oh, that didn't work."
- Tried filters — found "Flavor Notes" but the options were SCA terms she didn't recognize. "What's 'stone fruit'? Like... peaches?"
- Eventually found a bean by scrolling. Clicked into the detail page. "Oh wow, there's a lot of numbers here. Is 8.42 good? I have no idea."
- Saved it to her diary on the first try — "That was easy."
- **Key quote:** "I feel like this app is for people who already know about coffee. I'm still learning."

### P2 — Marcus, 35, home barista, owns espresso machine and V60
- Went straight to filters, applied "Washed" and "Ethiopia" — happy with the results
- On bean detail page: "I love that you have cupping scores. But can I compare two beans side by side? I'm trying to decide between these two."
- Tried to find a brew guide or recipe for a bean — "How should I brew this? I'd do pour-over for this one but it'd be nice if the app suggested that."
- Used the quiz — finished it but felt it was "too basic for me. The questions are fine for beginners though."
- **Key quote:** "The data is great. I just want to do more with it."

### P3 — Amy, 42, casual coffee drinker, uses French press daily
- Started with the quiz — enjoyed it, got her result. "This is fun! But now what? Where are the beans it recommended?"
- Clicked "Beans We Think You'll Love" but the link went to the full catalog, not a filtered view. "Wait, these aren't personalized? It just dumped me in the catalog."
- Tried the diary — added a bean, wrote a note. Came back later and couldn't find where her notes were. "Oh, I have to click into the bean again? I thought my notes would be on the diary page."
- **Key quote:** "The quiz got me excited and then the landing was a letdown."

### P4 — James, 24, coffee shop worker, knows SCA protocol
- Navigated confidently, used filters effectively
- On the homepage: "The 'Editor's Picks' section — what's the curation criteria? Are these high-scoring? Unique origins? I want to know why these were picked."
- Tried to share a bean with a coworker — no share functionality. "Can I just copy a link? Oh, the URL does work, that's fine I guess."
- Found the social feed — "Interesting, but who are these people? Are they real users or is this sample data?"
- **Key quote:** "I trust the data, but I want to understand the editorial choices."

### P5 — Priya, 31, tea drinker trying coffee, very new
- Confused by the homepage layout — "Is this a store? A blog? What am I supposed to do here?"
- Didn't notice the quiz link in the nav — found it only when prompted. "Oh, I wish that was more prominent. That would've been a better starting point for me."
- On bean detail: "Processing method: Washed. Okay, what does that mean for how it tastes?"
- Tried to use the diary but wasn't sure what "Want to Try" vs "Tried" meant in terms of how it affected her experience — "Does marking 'Want to Try' do anything, like save it somewhere special?"
- **Key quote:** "I need more hand-holding. Not in a bad way — just context for everything."

## Beta Feedback Survey (47 responses, key themes)

### What do you like most?
- "The cupping scores — nowhere else has this level of data" (12 mentions)
- "The quiz is fun and quick" (9 mentions)
- "The visual design is gorgeous, love the dark theme" (8 mentions)
- "The diary feature — I've been wanting to track my coffee" (7 mentions)

### What's most confusing?
- "The scores don't mean anything to me without context" (15 mentions)
- "I can't search by flavor, only by bean name" (11 mentions)
- "The quiz results don't connect well to the catalog" (8 mentions)
- "Not sure what some filter options mean" (7 mentions)
- "The difference between the homepage catalog and the beans page" (4 mentions)

### What's missing?
- "Brew guides or recipes" (14 mentions) ← We just built this!
- "Bean comparisons side by side" (9 mentions)
- "More explanation of what the scores mean" (8 mentions)
- "A way to find beans similar to ones I liked" (7 mentions)
- "Social sharing — I want to send beans to friends" (5 mentions)

### How likely are you to recommend? (NPS)
- Promoters (9-10): 38%
- Passives (7-8): 40%
- Detractors (0-6): 22%
- **NPS: +16** (decent for beta, room to grow)

### Selected open-ended responses
- "I love the concept but I feel like I need a coffee degree to use it properly."
- "Please add a 'why this bean is special' blurb. The numbers alone don't tell me the story."
- "The diary is great but I wish I could see my tasting notes without clicking into each bean."
- "Quiz → recommendations → buy should be a seamless funnel. Right now quiz → catalog feels disconnected."
- "Dark mode is chef's kiss. But some of the lighter text is hard to read."

## Slack Messages from the Team (informal)

**@design-lead:** "Users keep asking what the scores mean. We need contextual tooltips or a legend somewhere accessible."

**@dev-1:** "Three people in the beta channel mentioned they can't search by flavor. Our search only does bean name matching right now."

**@content-lead:** "The quiz results page feels like a dead end. People finish the quiz and don't know what to do next. The CTA sends them to the unfiltered catalog."

**@design-lead:** "Mobile testers are saying the filter bar overflows on smaller screens. They can't see all the options."

**@dev-2:** "Someone asked if 'Want to Try' in the diary actually does anything. It doesn't change recommendations or surface those beans anywhere. It's just a label right now."
