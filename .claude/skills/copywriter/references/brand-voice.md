# Brand Voice Guide — Coffee App

## Voice Summary
We sound like a knowledgeable friend who happens to love specialty coffee — warm, encouraging, never pretentious. We want people to feel excited about trying something new, not intimidated by jargon or scores.

## Tone Attributes
- **Warm** — like a good coffee shop on a cold morning. Welcoming, never cold or clinical.
- **Curious** — we're genuinely excited about flavor, origin, process. That curiosity is contagious.
- **Honest** — we don't oversell. If a bean is funky, we say so with a smile. If a score is average, that's fine — not every cup needs to be transcendent.
- **Encouraging** — the diary, the quiz, the discovery features — they all gently nudge. We celebrate exploration, not expertise.

## Progressive Jargon Rules
Copy depth should match page depth in the app:

| Layer | Pages | Jargon Level | Example |
|-------|-------|-------------|---------|
| Surface | Homepage, quiz, nav, CTAs | Plain language only | "Bright and fruity with a hint of chocolate" |
| Middle | Catalog, diary, filter labels | Light terminology with context | "Washed process — the clean, bright one" |
| Deep | Bean detail, cupping scores, advanced filters | Full SCA vocabulary | "Aroma 8.42 · Acidity 8.17 · Clean Cup 10.0" |

## Do / Don't

**Do:**
- Use "you" and "your" — it's personal
- Write in sentence case for headings (not Title Case everywhere)
- Use contractions (we're, you'll, it's)
- Describe flavors in everyday language first, technical second
- Make empty states feel like invitations, not dead ends
- Keep CTAs action-oriented and specific ("Find your next favorite" > "Submit")

**Don't:**
- Don't gatekeep — never imply someone needs to "know coffee" to enjoy this
- Don't over-emoji — one per empty state max, none in body copy
- Don't use "amazing" or "incredible" (the hero currently says "Amazing Coffee" — that's on the list to fix)
- Don't write marketing fluff with no substance ("elevate your coffee experience")
- Don't assume gender, dietary preferences, or brewing equipment

## Flavor Description Patterns
When describing a bean's flavor profile, lead with the sensory experience, not the score:

**Good:** "This Ethiopian natural tastes like biting into a ripe blueberry — sweet, a little winey, with a cocoa finish."

**Avoid:** "Scoring 87.42, this lot exhibits prominent fruity notes with satisfactory acidity."

## Page-Specific Voice Notes

### Homepage
Aspirational but grounded. The hero should make someone curious, not impressed. The catalog intro should feel like browsing a menu, not reading a database.

### Quiz
Playful and light. Short sentences. The quiz is the most casual part of the app — it's okay to be a little cheeky here.

### Bean Detail
This is where expertise lives. Technical terms are welcome, but always in service of helping someone understand what they're tasting. Cupping scores should have context ("8.5 is excellent — this bean has a remarkably clean finish").

### Diary
Intimate and reflective. This is the user's personal space. Copy should be quiet, supportive, and never performative. Empty states should feel like an open notebook, not a missing feature.

### Empty States & Errors
Always empathetic, never blame the user. Offer a clear next step.
- "No beans match those filters — try loosening up a bit?" ✓
- "No results found." ✗
